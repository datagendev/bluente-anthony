# Signal Types

Five categories of cross-border business signals that indicate a company may need document translation services -- specifically formatting-preserving translation (Bluente's strength).

---

## 1. Mergers & Acquisitions (cross-border)

**Why it matters**: Cross-border M&A generates massive volumes of legal, financial, and operational documents that need translation while preserving exact formatting -- contracts, regulatory filings, due diligence reports, board presentations.

**Evidence patterns**:
- Company A (country X) acquires Company B (country Y)
- Merger announced between companies operating in different language markets
- Cross-border acquisition of assets, divisions, or subsidiaries
- Private equity firm acquiring portfolio company in foreign market

**Urgency indicators**:
- Deal closing date mentioned (higher urgency if <90 days)
- Regulatory approval pending in foreign jurisdiction
- "Expected to close" language in press releases

**Translation likelihood signals**:
- Legal/financial documents involved (high -- always complex PDFs)
- Technology company (moderate -- depends on doc volume)
- Retail/consumer (lower -- more marketing, less complex docs)

**Scoring guidance**:
- Urgency: 8-10 if closing within 90 days, 5-7 if announced but no timeline, 3-4 if rumored
- Relevance: 8-10 for legal/financial M&A, 5-7 for tech acquisitions, 3-5 for small asset purchases
- Translation likelihood: 8-10 if multiple languages involved with complex docs, 5-7 if unclear doc types

---

## 2. International Expansion

**Why it matters**: Opening offices, entering new markets, or hiring internationally means HR docs, compliance materials, product documentation, and marketing collateral all need localization.

**Evidence patterns**:
- "Opening office in [foreign city]"
- "Expanding into [region/country]"
- "Launching operations in [market]"
- International hiring announcements (job posts in new countries)
- New country-specific website or domain registration

**Urgency indicators**:
- Office opening date announced
- Job postings already live in new market
- Press release with specific timeline

**Translation likelihood signals**:
- Regulated industry (pharma, finance, legal) -- high, compliance docs required
- SaaS/technology -- moderate, product and support docs
- Services company -- moderate, contracts and marketing

**Scoring guidance**:
- Urgency: 8-10 if actively hiring/opening office now, 5-7 if announced but future, 3-4 if industry reports only
- Relevance: 8-10 if entering market with different language, 5-7 if English-speaking market, 3-4 if minor expansion
- Translation likelihood: 8-10 if document-heavy industry, 5-7 if tech, 3-4 if services

---

## 3. Regulatory Compliance

**Why it matters**: New regulations often mandate multilingual documentation -- product labeling, safety data sheets, legal disclosures, patient information leaflets. These are typically complex, formatted documents.

**Evidence patterns**:
- New EU regulation requiring multilingual documentation
- Company subject to compliance requirements in multiple jurisdictions
- MDR (Medical Device Regulation) compliance activities
- Financial services compliance across borders (MiFID II, etc.)
- Environmental/sustainability reporting in multiple languages

**Urgency indicators**:
- Compliance deadline mentioned
- Penalties for non-compliance referenced
- Company publicly stating compliance timeline

**Translation likelihood signals**:
- Pharma/medical devices -- very high (SDS, IFU, labeling)
- Financial services -- high (disclosures, reports)
- Manufacturing -- high (safety docs, product manuals)
- Technology -- moderate (terms, privacy policies)

**Scoring guidance**:
- Urgency: 9-10 if compliance deadline <6 months, 6-8 if <12 months, 3-5 if ongoing
- Relevance: 9-10 if regulation explicitly requires multilingual docs, 6-8 if implied, 3-5 if tangential
- Translation likelihood: 9-10 for regulated docs (always complex PDFs), 6-8 for marketing compliance

---

## 4. Cross-Border Partnerships

**Why it matters**: Joint ventures, distribution agreements, licensing deals, and strategic partnerships between companies in different countries generate contracts, marketing co-creation, technical documentation, and operational manuals across languages.

**Evidence patterns**:
- Joint venture between companies in different countries
- Distribution agreement for new market
- Licensing deal across language boundaries
- Strategic partnership with international partner
- Channel partner announcements in foreign markets

**Urgency indicators**:
- Partnership already signed (documents being created now)
- Launch date for partnership activities
- Joint product launch announced

**Translation likelihood signals**:
- Legal agreements (always need precise translation)
- Technical documentation sharing
- Joint marketing materials
- Training/operational manuals

**Scoring guidance**:
- Urgency: 7-10 if partnership active/launching, 4-6 if announced but early, 2-4 if exploratory
- Relevance: 7-10 if document exchange is core to partnership, 4-6 if tangential, 2-4 if unclear
- Translation likelihood: 7-10 if legal/technical docs involved, 4-6 if marketing only, 2-4 if minimal docs

---

## 5. New Market Entry

**Why it matters**: Launching products or services in new geographies means product documentation, marketing materials, sales decks, support documentation, and legal terms all need localization. Often accompanied by localization job postings.

**Evidence patterns**:
- Product launch in new country/region
- Localization job posting (strong signal -- they're actively building capacity)
- New language added to product/website
- Country-specific marketing campaigns
- Trade show participation in new market

**Urgency indicators**:
- Launch date announced
- Localization manager job posting (means they need help NOW)
- Marketing campaign in production for new market

**Translation likelihood signals**:
- Product documentation heavy (manuals, specs, datasheets)
- Marketing collateral volume
- Legal/regulatory docs for new market
- Customer-facing content (support docs, FAQs)

**Scoring guidance**:
- Urgency: 8-10 if launching now or posting localization roles, 5-7 if announced future entry, 3-4 if exploring
- Relevance: 8-10 if document-heavy product, 5-7 if SaaS with minimal docs, 3-4 if services
- Translation likelihood: 8-10 if complex formatted docs (PDFs, manuals), 5-7 if mostly web content, 3-4 if minimal
