# Translation Need Indicators

How to identify whether a company has a genuine need for formatting-preserving document translation, by industry and signal type.

---

## Strong Indicators (high confidence)

- **Localization job posting**: Company is actively hiring for translation/localization. They need help NOW.
- **Regulatory filing in multiple jurisdictions**: Legal requirement for multilingual docs.
- **Cross-border M&A with legal documents**: Contracts must be translated precisely.
- **Medical device company entering EU**: MDR requires IFUs in all EU languages.
- **Company producing technical manuals for international markets**: Manuals have complex formatting.
- **Financial institution expanding internationally**: Regulatory disclosures required in local languages.

## Moderate Indicators (needs verification)

- **International office opening**: May need HR docs, contracts, operational manuals translated.
- **Partnership with foreign company**: Depends on partnership depth -- JVs need more docs than referral partnerships.
- **New language on website/product**: Shows localization activity, but web content is often simple.
- **International trade show participation**: May indicate market entry planning.
- **Hiring in foreign countries**: Employment contracts, benefits docs, policies need translation.

## Weak Indicators (proceed with caution)

- **Company has international customers but domestic operations**: May outsource translation already.
- **Remote employees in foreign countries**: Usually limited to HR docs, low volume.
- **Mentioned "going global" in blog post**: Aspirational, not actionable yet.
- **International investor/board member**: Financial docs only, limited scope.

---

## Document Type Assessment

When qualifying, estimate what types of documents the company likely needs translated:

| Document type | Formatting complexity | Bluente fit |
|--------------|----------------------|-------------|
| Legal contracts | High (headers, clauses, signatures, appendices) | Excellent |
| Regulatory filings | High (forms, tables, structured data) | Excellent |
| Product manuals | High (diagrams, specs, safety warnings, callouts) | Excellent |
| Financial reports | High (charts, tables, graphs, footnotes) | Excellent |
| Safety data sheets | High (standardized format, tables, hazard symbols) | Excellent |
| Marketing brochures | Moderate-High (layouts, images, brand elements) | Good |
| Presentations | Moderate (slides, charts, formatted text) | Good |
| HR policies | Moderate (headers, lists, tables) | Good |
| Web content | Low (plain text, simple formatting) | Poor fit |
| Short strings (UI) | None | Not a fit |
| Chat messages | None | Not a fit |

Focus qualification on companies that produce documents in the "High" formatting complexity range.
