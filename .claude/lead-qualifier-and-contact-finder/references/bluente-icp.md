# Bluente ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

Reference for qualifying signals against Bluente's ideal customer.

---

## Company Characteristics

### Must-have
- **Cross-border activity**: Active or expanding international operations
- **Document production**: Regularly produces PDFs, contracts, compliance docs, or marketing materials
- **Translation need**: Documents cross language boundaries (not just English-to-English)

### Ideal
- **Size**: 50-5000 employees
  - Large enough to have real translation volume
  - Small enough to NOT have a dedicated localization team with existing TMS
- **Industry**: Legal, financial services, manufacturing, pharma/medical devices, technology, consulting
- **Document types**: Complex formatted PDFs (charts, tables, layouts, diagrams)
- **Language pairs**: Multiple target languages (higher value per deal)
- **Current solution**: Manual translation, basic MT (Google Translate), or no solution at all

### Disqualifiers
- **< 20 employees**: Unlikely to have enough volume
- **Purely domestic**: No international signals whatsoever
- **Has dedicated localization team + TMS**: Already using SDL Trados, memoQ, Smartling, Phrase -- unlikely to switch unless unhappy
- **Government/military**: Long procurement cycles, bad fit for early-stage company
- **Translation/localization companies**: Competitors, not prospects
- **Simple content only**: If the company only needs short text strings or web content translated, Bluente's formatting-preservation value prop doesn't apply

## Contact Hierarchy

Who owns the translation workflow, in priority order:

| Priority | Title Pattern | Why |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| 1 | Localization Manager, Translation Lead, Localization Director | Direct owner of translation workflows |
| 2 | Legal Operations Manager, Head of Legal Ops | Owns legal document workflows (M&A, compliance) |
| 3 | International Operations, Global Operations, VP International | Owns cross-border operational workflows |
| 4 | Content Operations, Marketing Operations, Head of Content | Owns marketing localization |
| 5 | COO, VP Operations, Head of Operations | At smaller companies, catch-all for operational needs |

## Industry Fit Scoring

| Industry | Fit | Document types | Notes |
|----------|-----|---------------|-------|
| Legal/Law firms | Excellent | Contracts, filings, agreements | Formatting is critical for legal docs |
| Financial services | Excellent | Reports, disclosures, compliance docs | Regulatory requires precise formatting |
| Pharma/Medical devices | Excellent | IFUs, SDS, labeling, regulatory submissions | MDR compliance is a major driver |
| Manufacturing | Strong | Product manuals, safety docs, specs | Technical documentation with diagrams |
| Technology/SaaS | Good | Product docs, API docs, marketing | Volume depends on international presence |
| Consulting | Good | Reports, presentations, proposals | Client-facing docs need polish |
| Education | Moderate | Course materials, certifications | Volume varies widely |
| Retail/E-commerce | Moderate | Product descriptions, marketing | Often simpler content, less formatting |

## Translation Volume Estimators

Use these rough heuristics:

| Signal type | Estimated translation volume | Time pressure |
|------------|----------------------------|---------------|
| Cross-border M&A | 500-5000 pages | High (deal timeline) |
| International expansion | 100-1000 pages initially, ongoing stream | Moderate |
| Regulatory compliance | 200-2000 pages | High (compliance deadline) |
| Cross-border partnership | 50-500 pages | Moderate |
| New market entry | 200-1000 pages | Moderate to high |
