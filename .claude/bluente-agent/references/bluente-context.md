# Bluente Context

## The Product

Bluente is a document translation platform that preserves PDF formatting across 120+ languages. When you put in a beautifully formatted PDF with charts, tables, and complex layouts and translate it (e.g., English to Japanese, English to Arabic), it comes out with the formatting intact -- no broken layouts, no manual reformatting.

This has been a significant pain point in the market. Most translation tools either strip formatting or produce garbled output that requires hours of manual cleanup.

## Why Cross-Border Signals

Companies showing cross-border activity are Bluente's highest-intent prospects because:
1. **M&A deals** generate thousands of pages of legal, compliance, and operational documents that need translation
2. **International expansion** means product docs, marketing collateral, HR policies, and contracts all need localization
3. **Regulatory compliance** in new markets often requires multilingual documentation
4. **Cross-border partnerships** produce contracts, marketing materials, and technical specs crossing language boundaries
5. **New market entry** means websites, product manuals, sales decks, and support docs need translation

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Company characteristics
- **Size**: 50-5000 employees (large enough to have translation volume, small enough to not have an in-house localization team)
- **Activity**: Active cross-border operations or expanding internationally
- **Document volume**: Produces PDFs, contracts, compliance docs, marketing materials regularly
- **Current solution**: Manual translation, basic MT (Google Translate), or expensive legacy TMS
- **Industries**: Legal, financial services, manufacturing, pharma, technology, consulting

### Contact hierarchy (who owns the translation need)
1. Localization Manager / Translation Lead
2. Legal Operations Manager
3. International Operations / Global Ops
4. Content / Marketing Operations (for marketing-heavy use cases)
5. COO / VP Operations (at smaller companies)

### Disqualification criteria
- Fewer than 20 employees (unlikely to have enough translation volume)
- Purely domestic operations with no international signals
- Already using a major TMS (SDL Trados, memoQ, Smartling) with dedicated localization team
- Government/military (long procurement cycles, not ideal for early-stage)
